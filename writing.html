Self-help

Woo woo' or a genuine drive to be better

March 02, 2024 Me

Being into 'self-help' is tricky. It's hard to tell your friends or family without them rolling their eyes or giving you a side-eyed look. I've found the best way to tell people you're into self-help is not use words. Use action. Tweek your habits and lifestyle to serve you better, look at the details and make 1% adjustments each week. Soon you'll be showing your love ones an improved version of you without mentioning who you're influenced by. For anyone who is struggling to dive in and give self-help a shot. I've listed 5 reasons why I follow this content for close to a decade now, and I don't seem to want to slow down any time soon. 1) Awareness. I like keeping an eye on any seed of an idea I can implement in my own life; and asking can I use this? More often than I'd like, I feel like I know less and less about living the good life, and quickly turn to others to hear they have to say. 2) Perspective. I love hearing the interesting lives people live throughout the world. It broadens my view of what is possible. I'm fascinated in high-performers think differently; reframing ideas to guide them down a better path. 3) Common traits. The common through-line for most high performers I've noticed so far are: - A morning workout to start the day - The daily practice of writing - The ability to communicate clearly and think critically. - Executing at a large scale when others can't or won't. - The thirst for knowledge never stops. Always reading, and always learning. 4) The craft. Repetition doesn't mean you'll always get better. Practice doesn't make perfect; you can repeat bad habits and never get better. It's possible to drive a car for a living and be the worst driver in the country. So, if I'm able to be aware of better practices, it puts me in a better place to tweek my habits along the way. 5) Accountability. Any high performer has a coach. If they think they are going to be in the game a long time. They invest in coaches to help them. Asking for help is a strength not a weakness. I've found this discovery enlightening and helpful in my life. I find it such a shame there is such a barrier to entry for so many. For anyone who finds these findings interesting, remember; you can start straight away, but don't tell your friends about it, just show them a better you.
